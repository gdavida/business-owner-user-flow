Phase 1: Remote Outreach Strategy

Approach: Start with efficient, low-touch methods to capture early adopters and create initial momentum

Timeline: Week 1-2 of outreach campaign

Success metric: If you get 15-20 core businesses from Phase 1, you can launch. If not, escalate to Phase 2.

Phase 2: Physical Escalation Strategy

When to use: Phase 1 didn't reach minimum launch threshold (15-20 businesses)

Target: Must-have businesses that didn't respond to remote outreach

ROI consideration: Higher time investment but much higher conversion rates

Complete Business Journey

Overview: Full journey from initial outreach through successful page enhancement

Strategy: Phase 1 for broad reach, Phase 2 for targeted conversion, followed by gradual enhancement

Timeline: 2-4 weeks initial launch, ongoing enhancement prompts over months

PHASE 1: REMOTE OUTREACH (Start Here)
Mass Outreach
Newsletter/Facebook/Email Blast
"New Business Directory Launching! Join 50+ local businesses already signed up. Click here to get listed."
"Another chamber announcement... I'll look at this later" (but probably won't)
Friction: Easy to ignore, no urgency, gets buried in other messages, feels like spam
Personal Call/Text
Champion Calls Your Business Directly
"Hi Sarah, this is [Name] from the chamber. I'm calling because we're launching a directory to help tourists find businesses like yours..."
"Oh, [Name] is calling me personally. This must be important."
Success factor: Personal relationship, feels important, immediate Q&A opportunity
Peer Recommendation
Business Owner Tells You Directly
"Hey, you should join the town directory - I got three customers from it this month and it was super easy!"
"If it worked for [Business], and they're telling me directly, it must actually work"
Power factor: Peer trust + specific success story + personal recommendation
Self Discovery
Finds Directory Online
Discovers directory while researching local competition, sees other businesses listed, or finds it through Google search.
"This looks professional and my competitors are on here. How do I get listed?"
Advantage: Self-motivated, proactive, already convinced of value
EVALUATION PHASE
Mass Psychology
Low Engagement Decision
Business owner gives it minimal attention. Quick scan to decide if worth investigating further.
"Is this another thing that costs money? Do I have time to figure this out?"
Major barrier: No immediate way to get questions answered, assumes complexity/cost
Personal Psychology
High Engagement Decision
Real conversation happens. Questions get answered immediately. Champion builds confidence and urgency.
"Since [Name] took the time to call me personally, this must be legitimate"
Conversion driver: Immediate objection handling, relationship trust, can ask questions
High Intent
Already Pre-Convinced
Evaluation is quick because trust/value is already established through peer recommendation or self-research.
"I already know I want to do this, just need to figure out how"
Speed factor: Skips skepticism phase, moves quickly to action
ACTION DECISION PHASE
Mass: Proceed
Clicks Link & Starts Form
The small percentage who are immediately interested or self-motivated click through and start the form.
"Let me see what this is about... oh it's actually pretty simple"
Self-selector trait: Early adopters, tech-comfortable, already convinced of digital marketing value
Personal: Proceed
Agrees During Call
Champion addresses concerns in real-time, provides form link, often offers to help with completion.
"If [Champion] says it's only 10 minutes and other businesses are doing it..."
Conversion drivers: Personal accountability, immediate support offer, objection handling
High Intent: Proceed
Enthusiastic Yes
Quick agreement because value is already proven (word-of-mouth) or self-evident (self-discovery).
Fast track: Minimal friction, often complete form same day
PHASE 2: PHYSICAL ESCALATION (When Phase 1 Response is Low)
Physical Visit
Champion Visits Business In-Person
Champion shows up with tablet and forms. "Hi, I'm [Name] from the chamber. I wanted to stop by personally about our new business directory..."
"Wow, they came here personally? This must be really important."
Power factor: Physical presence = maximum urgency, immediate completion, relationship building
Direct Mail
Professional Mailer + QR Code
High-quality postcard with local business photos, QR code for easy form access, Champion's direct phone number for support.
"This looks official and professional. Other businesses are really on here."
Advantage: Physical mail harder to ignore than email, QR code reduces friction, professional appearance
High Pressure Decision
Hard to Say No in Person
Champion is right there, can answer questions immediately, form gets completed on the spot with help.
"They took the time to come here personally - I should at least listen."
Immediate completion: Champion fills out form together, takes photos on-site, no follow-up needed
Physical Urgency
Mailer Feels More Official
Physical mail implies investment and importance. QR code makes response easy, Champion's phone number provides support.
"They spent money to mail this to me specifically. Maybe I should check it out."
Still some friction: Need to scan QR code, still requires self-motivation, may delay action
INITIAL FORM & BASIC PAGE (Quick Launch)
Initial Form
Basic Info to Get Started
Essential details only: business name, address, phone, hours, one photo, short description. Designed for quick completion.
"Oh, this really is just 10 minutes - just the basics to get me listed"
Low friction design: Minimal required fields, get them live fast, enhance later
Basic Page Live
Simple Directory Listing Goes Live
Professional but basic page with core business info. Functional and findable, but room to grow.
"Great, I'm on there! This looks good and I can always add more later."
Quick win: Business owner sees immediate value, builds confidence in the platform
ENHANCEMENT JOURNEY (Level Up Over Time)
Enhancement Prompts
Gentle Nudges to Level Up
Soft prompts via email: "Add more photos," "Include special offers," "Tell your story." Less aggressive than initial outreach.
"I should probably add more details since people are actually finding me through this"
Post-launch timing: Business owner has seen value, more willing to invest time in improvements
Enhanced Page
Full-Featured Business Profile
Complete listing with photo gallery, detailed descriptions, special offers, events, reviews, social links.
"Now this really showcases what we're all about - looks amazing!"
Advocacy amplified: Enhanced businesses become the strongest referral sources and success stories

Two-Tier Success Strategy

Tier 1: Get businesses live quickly with basic info (high completion rate)

Tier 2: Enhance over time when they see value (higher engagement rate)

Note: Detailed enhancement journey & strategies covered in separate workflow

Phase 1 vs Phase 2: Strategic Escalation

Phase 1: Remote Outreach (Start Here)

Mass Outreach

Reach: High volume
Champion Time: Very efficient
Response: Lower engagement
Who Responds: Early adopters

Personal Outreach

Reach: Lower volume
Champion Time: Time intensive
Response: Higher engagement
Who Responds: Skeptical businesses

Phase 2: Physical Escalation (When Phase 1 is Insufficient)

Physical Visits

When to Use: Key businesses only
Champion Time: Very high per business
Response: Highest conversion
Advantage: Immediate completion

Direct Mail

When to Use: Broad re-engagement
Champion Time: Design + mail prep
Response: Higher than email
Advantage: Professional appearance

Strategic Decision Framework

Start with Phase 1: Mass outreach + selective personal calls capture early adopters efficiently

Evaluate after 2 weeks: If less than 15-20 core businesses signed up, escalate to Phase 2

Phase 2 targeting: Focus on must-have businesses (restaurants, main street retail, visitor services)

ROI consideration: Physical visits worth it for key businesses, direct mail for broader re-engagement
